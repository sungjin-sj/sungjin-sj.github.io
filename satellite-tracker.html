Satellite Tracker Project

Motion, Interaction, and UI/UX Design

Technologies Used

Solidworks C++ / Arduino 3D Printing Electronics Stepper Motors

Project Overview

The goal of this project was to design and prototype a high-precision, dual-axis (Azimuth-Elevation) motorized ground station. The system is specifically engineered to track Sun-Synchronous Orbit (SSO) satellites, which travel at approximately 7.5 km/s and require high-slew rate tracking and precise pointing to maintain stable communication links. The structure was designed to support a heavy 1.5 kg Yagi antenna boom.

Functional Capabilities

  • Real-time Tracking: Adjusts azimuth and elevation in real-time based on TLE data.
  • Autonomous Operation: Operates independently once coordinates are uploaded.
  • High Precision: Utilizes stepper motors with micro-stepping for precise pointing accuracy.

Mechanical Engineering & Design

To ensure tracking stability under environmental disturbances, a comprehensive mechanical trade study was performed to analyze bearing loads and required torque.

  • Actuation System: NEMA-23 stepper motors were selected for both axes.
  • Gear Reduction: The system utilizes a 5:1 gear ratio for Azimuth and a 60:1 worm gear reduction ratio for Elevation.
  • Prototyping: The assembly was designed using SolidWorks and fabricated through 3D printing to ensure structural integrity and rapid iteration.

Design Choices

The structure was designed in SolidWorks and fully 3D printed to allow for rapid iterative testing of mechanical tolerances.

  • Non-Backdrivable Elevation Axis: A 60:1 worm gear reduction ratio was specifically chosen for the elevation axis. This ensures the system is not back-drivable, preventing environmental disturbances (such as high wind loads on the 1.5 kg Yagi antenna) from back-driving the motor and losing tracking alignment when powered down.

Assembly & Parts

The assembly features custom-designed gears and bearing mounts to ensure smooth rotation under load.

  • Integrated Sensor Suite: A 6DoF IMU (Inertial Measurement Unit) is integrated via I2C to provide real-time magnetometer, accelerometer, and gyroscope feedback. This allows the system to verify its physical orientation against the calculated TLE coordinates.

Electrical Architecture & Power Logic

The electrical system was designed with a focus on signal integrity and power management.

  • Power Conversion: A dedicated power system converts 220 VAC to 24 VDC to power the Motorbank MSD-225 drivers.
  • Control Unit: An Arduino Mega 2560 manages the system logic.
  • Signal Isolation: The controller handles 5V TTL logic for Pulse (PUL), Direction (DIR), and Enable (ENA) signals.
  • Isolation Strategy: This architecture ensures complete electrical isolation between the high-power motors and the sensitive logic controllers to prevent interference.

Software Implementation & Control Tuning

The tracking intelligence is driven by embedded software developed in C++/Arduino.

  • Pass Prediction: The software implements orbital propagation algorithms to parse Two-Line Element (TLE) data.
  • Real-Time Coordinates: By calculating real-time Azimuth and Elevation coordinates, the system autonomously predicts satellite passes and adjusts the antenna position.
  • Dynamic Response: Jitter-free movement was achieved by iteratively tuning the control algorithms and motor micro-stepping.
