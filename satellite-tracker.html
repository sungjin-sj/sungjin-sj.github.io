Satellite Tracker Project

Motion, Interaction, and UI/UX Design

Project Video Demo

A demonstration of my technical work using motion and interaction.

Project Requirements & Capabilities

The primary goal of this project was to design and build a 2-axis satellite tracker capable of autonomously tracking low-earth orbit satellites.

Functional Capabilities

  • Real-time Tracking: Adjusts azimuth and elevation in real-time based on TLE data.
  • Autonomous Operation: Operates independently once coordinates are uploaded.
  • High Precision: Utilizes stepper motors with micro-stepping for precise pointing accuracy.

System Architecture

The system is divided into three main subsystems: The Mechanical Structure, The Control Electronics, and The Software Interface.

Mechanical Design & Assembly

Design Choices

The structure was designed in Solidworks and fully 3D printed. A key design choice was the use of a balanced gimbal mechanism to minimize the torque required by the elevation motor.

Assembly & Parts

The assembly features custom-designed gears and bearing mounts to ensure smooth rotation under load.

Electronics & Wiring

The electronics stack consists of an Arduino microcontroller driving two stepper drivers.

Cable management was handled through the hollow center shaft to prevent tangling during 360-degree rotation.

Technologies Used

Solidworks C++ / Arduino 3D Printing Electronics Stepper Motors
